With regard to the Ethereum network, "gas" is a unit of measurement for the amount of computing power needed to execute a transaction on the EVM. When you send a transaction on the network, a certain amount of gas is used. Each block has a gas limit to prevent denial-of-service attacks and smart contract errors.

When you send a transaction, you must pay for the gas that your transaction uses. In effect, you're paying the network for the computation required to complete it. Gas prices fluctuate based on the previous block's gas limit and the gas target, all of which is dependent on current network congestion and demand.

EIP-1559 is an Ethereum standard that changed how Ethereum 1.0 transactions work. It made them more efficient, and changed the pricing mechanism. You can read more about it here.
